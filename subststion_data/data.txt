1. transformer.jpg
Equipment
33/11 kV Power Transformer (Toshiba)
Purpose
This is the primary power conversion backbone of the substation. It steps down the high-voltage transmission-level electricity of 33,000 Volts (33 kV) from the incoming utility grid line down to a medium-voltage distribution-level of 11,000 Volts (11 kV). This step-down process is critical for localized power distribution, enabling safe routing through local distribution feeders. By converting the voltage, it significantly reduces current levels for long-distance transport and prepares the electrical energy for localized secondary transformers closer to end-consumers. It serves as the single most critical asset in the substation, with its operational health directly impacting the availability of the entire local power grid.

Functional Notes
The external cooling radiators dissipate thermal energy generated by core and winding resistive losses.
The conservator tank accommodates the expansion and contraction of insulating mineral oil during load changes.
The Buchholz relay is a gas-actuated safety device designed to detect internal faults (arcing, overheating, or insulation breakdown) by capturing accumulated gas bubbles.
The dehydrating breather extracts moisture from the air drawn into the conservator tank during thermal breathing cycles.
Winding and oil temperature monitoring sensors trigger cooling fans at preset limits and actuate protective trips if safe limits are exceeded.
